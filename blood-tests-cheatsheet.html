Core Cardiovascular Intelligence
Test 01 · Cardiovascular Critical Priority
ApoB
Apolipoprotein B
What It Measures
ApoB counts the actual number of dangerous lipid particles in your blood — not their weight. Every ApoB particle can penetrate arterial walls and trigger plaque formation. LDL cholesterol (what standard panels test) measures particle weight, missing particle count entirely.
Why Doctors Don't Order It Two people with identical LDL can have 3× different ApoB levels — and 3× different cardiac risk. Standard panels were designed pre-2000. ApoB wasn't widely validated until 2006.

Test 02 · Metabolic Critical Priority
Fasting
Insulin
Serum Insulin, Fasting
What It Measures
Insulin resistance — the root cause of type 2 diabetes, metabolic syndrome, and a major driver of cardiovascular disease and Alzheimer's. Fasting glucose (what standard panels test) only rises after your pancreas is already significantly impaired. Fasting insulin rises 10–15 years before glucose moves.
Optimal
< 6 µIU/mL
Insulin sensitive
"Normal"
< 25 µIU/mL
Includes pre-diabetes
Why Doctors Miss It Fasting glucose is the standard screen. By the time glucose is elevated, insulin has been dangerously high for years. The "normal" range is calibrated to the average — which includes a population already trending toward metabolic disease.
Say To Your Doctor "Can we add fasting insulin to my metabolic panel? I want an early view of insulin sensitivity."
Test 03 · Inflammation High Priority
hs-CRP
High-Sensitivity C-Reactive Protein
What It Measures
Chronic systemic inflammation — the biological root mechanism behind heart disease, most cancers, Alzheimer's, and autoimmune conditions. Standard CRP detects acute infection. hs-CRP is 10× more sensitive, catching the low-grade silent inflammation that causes slow, decades-long cellular damage.
Optimal
< 1.0 mg/L
Low inflammatory state
"Normal"
< 3.0 mg/L
Too permissive for prevention
Why Doctors Miss It Standard CRP is ordered when infection is suspected — not for silent inflammation screening. Most GPs never distinguish between standard and high-sensitivity versions of this test.
Say To Your Doctor "I'd like high-sensitivity CRP specifically — not standard CRP — as an inflammation marker."
Genetic Cardiac Risk — Test Once in a Lifetime
Test 04 · Genetic Cardiovascular Test Once — Lifetime Data
Lp(a)
Lipoprotein Little-a
A genetically determined lipoprotein present in elevated form in ~20% of the population. Elevated Lp(a) triples cardiovascular risk — even in people with perfect cholesterol, optimal ApoB, and healthy lifestyle. It is entirely genetic. It cannot be modified by diet or exercise. But knowing about it changes everything about how you manage risk.

Arterial & Brain Health
Test 05 · Vascular & Cognitive Essential Priority
Homocysteine
Plasma Total Homocysteine
An amino acid that, when elevated, directly damages arterial walls and accelerates cognitive decline. Strongly linked to heart attack, stroke, and Alzheimer's disease. The critical point: elevated homocysteine is almost always caused by B-vitamin deficiencies (B6, B9, B12) — making it one of the most fixable risks on this list.
Arterial damage Cognitive decline Stroke risk B-vitamin status
Optimal
< 9 µmol/L
Arterially protective
"Normal"
< 15 µmol/L

Why Doctors Miss It Almost never ordered unless a patient has already had a cardiovascular event. Elevated homocysteine is treatable with B-vitamin supplementation — but only if you know to look for it. Damage from unchecked high homocysteine is not reversible.
If Elevated — Immediate Action
  • Methylfolate (B9) 400–800 mcg/day
  • Methylcobalamin (B12) 500–1000 mcg/day
  • Pyridoxine (B6) 10–25 mg/day
  • Retest in 90 days — levels normalize quickly
Say To Your Doctor "Can we add plasma homocysteine to my panel? I want to check my arterial and cognitive risk markers."
